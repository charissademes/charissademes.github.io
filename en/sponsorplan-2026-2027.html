1. Who is Charissa and why this sponsor plan?

Hi, I am Charissa. I am 17 years old and I study bachelor PABO to become a teacher in Alkmaar.

My hobbies are meeting friends, cooking and baking. But one hobby stands head and shoulders above the rest: speed skating. I have been skating since I was 7 years old. On my strap-on skates, wearing a thick coat and a hat, I was at the ice rink in Alkmaar every Saturday morning for youth skating.

About 5 years ago it was time for the next step: competitive speed skating. I started relatively late and, as an A-pupil, found myself among skaters who were much faster and stronger than I was. Winning was never my goal, though. I enjoyed the training sessions, and that was enough for me. I started to enjoy skating more and more and began training more often. Where I first trained once a week, I now went to the rink 3 times a week. This helped me make big progress, and during my 2nd competition season I was allowed to train with the selection group. That paid off, and soon I was invited to compete in regional races.

This quickly meant that I no longer enjoyed only the training, but also skating fast times and measuring myself against others my age. In my 3rd competition season I went to Thialf for the first time to experience the fast ice in Heerenveen. That had a positive effect, because in my 4th season I was invited to join the track selection team. A big and exciting step. At the start of the season I never expected to compete in important races. Then, in early December, I was a reserve for the national qualification race for the Dutch Allround Championships, and in February I qualified directly through the ranking for the national qualification race for both the Dutch Sprint Championships and the Dutch Long Distance Championships. I ended the season as 1st reserve for the Dutch Sprint Championships.

Although I had not expected to compete in Dutch Championships, it was still a tough pill to swallow. With a new goal in mind, I gave everything last season to get the best out of myself. I wanted only one thing: to qualify for the Dutch Allround Championships. We started training in October, and in early January I was on the start line of the national qualification race for the Dutch Allround Championships. This takes place over a weekend in which all participants skate 3 distances. Who ultimately qualifies for the Dutch Championships depends on the final ranking. Both days went well for me, which meant I had already achieved my goal within the first 2 months of the season. That was no reason for me to sit back. Several Dutch Championships are held during a season.

In February I again had a race weekend on the schedule in which I had to skate 3 distances. This time it was for 2 different Dutch Championships. On Saturday the sprint spots were awarded and on Sunday the long-distance spots. Unfortunately, I did not feel well on Saturday, so I only qualified as a reserve for the Dutch Sprint Championships. My full focus was then on the long distances. I managed to qualify. Because others withdrew from the sprint, I was still able to skate that event as well. The Dutch Sprint Championships and the Dutch Long Distance Championships always take place in the same weekend, so I had a busy weekend ahead with 5 distances on the schedule.

I finished the season by competing in 3 individual Dutch Championships. This earned me a talent status awarded by NOC*NSF to talented young athletes. For the coming season I have set myself another goal: to skate Dutch Championships again.

2. Why sponsorship is needed

The road to competing at Dutch Championships is demanding, and not only physically. Speed skating is an expensive sport. In addition to club membership fees, season passes, costs for the track selection team, race fees and training camps, there is a lot involved. As a skater, you need to purchase many items, such as skates, a bike, aerodynamic suits, inline skates and suitable footwear. Maintaining the equipment is expensive as well. In short, to achieve this goal I need support through sponsorship.
